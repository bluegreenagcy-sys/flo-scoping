Flo Rebrand - Phase 1 & 2 Strategy.

How we're structuring the project, why we split it, and exactly what we're building - and not building.

Prepared by Asan · BlueGreen
Client Michael Hewitt · Flo (WalletFlo)
Date May 2026
Status Pre-contract · Alignment
01

The Situation

Where We Are
Michael and the Flo team came back with a scope change after reviewing the original deliverables. They want more than a surface identity refresh - they want a content engine that positions Flo for launch and fuels 2–3 months of social growth. That's a different project. And it needs to be structured differently.
-
They came back with a modified scope
Requested 25 ready-to-publish assets, GIFs similar to Phantom's X posts, TikTok cinematic clips, AI character videos, and UI application of the new brand system.
-
The name is changing
WalletFlo is being rebranded to Flo. That's confirmed. This isn't a visual refresh - it's a full rebrand. The name, the identity, the positioning. All of it.
-
They referenced Phantom, Polymarket, Robinhood, Coinbase, Whop
Rebellious. Premium. Pop-culture oriented. Video-first. Designed for today's attention span. Not another boring finance brand.
02

The Problem with the Current Ask

Hard Truth
25 unique, fully produced 15–30 second videos is a six-figure project. That's not what's on the table. And that's not actually what they need - they need a content system that keeps them fed for months. Those are two very different things.
01
25 unique videos ≠ 25 unique productions
A single polished 30-second video ad - scripted, designed, animated, with sound - can cost $2,000–$5,000 on its own. 25 of them is $50k–$100k minimum. We need a smarter model.
02
Brand identity can't be applied before it's finalized
If we produce 25 social assets before the logo and color system is approved - and then the logo changes - we redo everything. That's not a plan, that's a liability.
03
AI character videos are a separate project entirely
Using real influencer likenesses in AI-generated video has legal implications, requires separate tooling, and cannot be bundled quietly into Phase 2. That's its own conversation.
03

The Solution - Two Phases

Splitting the project into two sequential phases creates a logical dependency that protects both sides. Phase 2 cannot begin until Phase 1 is approved and locked. This is how every serious branding project should work.

Phase 1
Brand Identity & Visual System
  • Complete logo suite - all formats and variations
  • Color system with HEX, RGB, CMYK values
  • Typography hierarchy and usage rules
  • Character mascot (like Phantom's ghost)
  • Art direction and brand personality
  • Real-world application mockups (billboards, signage)
  • UI application: color + typography only on 5 screens
Phase 2
Content Production & Creative Assets
  • 3 hero promotional videos (30–40 sec each)
  • Each hero repurposed into 4 content versions
  • = 12 video pieces total, nothing redundant
  • 10 additional static assets (images, GIFs, memes)
  • = 25 ready-to-publish pieces total
  • All built on the finalized Phase 1 identity
Why This Protects Everyone
If a logo or color direction shifts in Phase 1, we don't redo 25 social assets. We finalize the look - then apply and implement it through the content we produce. That's not delivering less. That's delivering smarter.
04

Phase 1 - Scope in Detail

Brand Identity

1.1
Complete Logo Suite As agreed
Primary logo, all variations, monochrome, reverse, favicon. All vector and raster formats.
1.2
Color & Typography Systems As agreed
Color palette with all values. Typeface selections with hierarchy and usage rules.
1.3
Character Mascot New addition
A brand character in the spirit of Phantom's ghost - ownable, memorable, and built to carry the Flo personality across content. Needs to be scoped and priced separately from original agreement.
1.4
Real-World Brand Application Replacing Brand Book
Billboards, signage mockups - showing how Flo lives in the world. This replaces the full brand guidelines document they deprioritized. Shows the identity in context, not in documentation.

UI Application

Important Boundary
BlueGreen is applying the brand to the app - not redesigning the app. This means color and typography updates only. No UX changes, no layout shifts, no component redesigns. We show how it looks. We do not hand off production-ready developer files.
1.5
5 App Screens Included Priced by screen
We apply new colors and typography to 5 key screens. Additional screens beyond 5 are charged separately. Scope of which 5 screens to be confirmed before Phase 1 begins.
05

Phase 2 - Scope in Detail

Phase 2 begins only after Phase 1 is fully approved. The goal: give Flo a content system that covers 2–3 months post-launch without running dry.

The 3 Hero Videos

V1
The Launch Video
Announces the new app, the rebrand, and the rename to Flo. Designed to go wide - the intro of a new era for the brand.
V2
Selling Point A - Brand Awareness
Highlights a key product feature that builds brand awareness and positions Flo in the market. To be defined with Michael and Spencer.
V3
Selling Point B - Community & Following
Highlights a feature or angle built to drive social following and community growth. More playful, more shareable.

The Repurposing Model

3
Hero Videos
30–40 sec each
Fully produced. Scripted, designed, animated. One per core message.
×4
Per Video
4 repurposed versions
With voiceover / without voiceover / looping cut / short cut. Same core, different format.
25
Total Pieces
12 videos + 10 static
12 repurposed video pieces + 10 images, GIFs, and memes. All ready to post. Nothing redundant.

The Other 10

Static & Playful Assets
The remaining 10 pieces are static assets - designed images, short looping silent video clips (what they're calling "GIFs"), and meme-style posts in Flo's tone. Rebellious, premium, pop-culture oriented. The kind of content Phantom and Polymarket run on X.

The Approval Workflow

→
Step 1 - Storyboard all 3 hero videos. Get approval.
Nothing goes into production without a green light on the concept first.
→
Step 2 - Produce the 3 hero videos. Get approval.
Final motion, sound, and graphics. Locked and approved before repurposing begins.
→
Step 3 - Repurpose into 12 pieces. Produce the 10 static assets.
All derivative content comes from the approved heroes. 25 pieces total. Done.
06

Why This Is the Right System

"They think they want 25 different videos. What they need is a system that ensures they don't run out of content for 2–3 months after launch."
✓
Budget-aligned
3 hero productions is achievable within the current project budget. 25 unique productions is not. This gets them to 25 pieces without blowing the scope.
✓
This is how reference brands actually operate
Phantom, Robinhood, Coinbase, Polymarket - none of them are producing 25 unique videos per cycle. They produce a few hero pieces and repurpose intelligently across platforms.
✓
Protects against rework
Phase 1 locks the identity before Phase 2 produces anything. No color changes, no logo tweaks mid-production. Every asset is built on an approved foundation.
